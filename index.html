Supply & Operations
**Supply & Operations** The most significant supply-side constraint currently affecting physical barrels is the ongoing underinvestment in upstream oil production capacity. Despite a rebound in demand, many oil-producing nations and companies are facing challenges in ramping up production due to a lack of new exploration and development projects. This situation is particularly pronounced in regions such as the North Sea and parts of the U.S. shale sector, where existing infrastructure is aging and new investments have been limited over the past year. As a result, the ability to increase output in response to rising demand is constrained, leading to tighter market conditions.

Overall, gasoline stands out as the key driver in the current market landscape, with its strength overshadowing the softer signals from diesel.
