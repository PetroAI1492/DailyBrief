Key Decisions & Watchpoints
If geopolitical tensions in key oil-producing regions persist, then supply disruptions may lead to increased volatility in crude oil prices. If refinery utilization rates remain low due to maintenance activities, then product inventories could build, potentially exerting downward pressure on gasoline prices. If global demand for petroleum products continues to show signs of weakness, then investment in upstream projects may decline further, impacting future supply capabilities.
